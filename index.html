Environmental Crisis Facts

Climate Change

The Earth's temperature has risen by 1.1°C since the late 1800s, with the last decade being the warmest on record. This warming is causing extreme weather events, rising sea levels, and ecosystem disruptions.

Deforestation

Every year, we lose approximately 10 million hectares of forest—an area about the size of Iceland. Forests are crucial carbon sinks, and their destruction accounts for about 10% of global warming.

Ocean Acidification

The oceans absorb about 30% of emitted CO2, causing acidification that threatens marine life, especially organisms with shells or skeletons like corals and shellfish.

Simple Ways to Reduce Your Impact

Daily Actions That Make a Difference

  • Switch to renewable energy: If possible, choose a green energy provider or consider installing solar panels.
  • Optimize transportation: Use public transport, carpool, bike, or walk when possible. Electric vehicles produce significantly fewer emissions.
  • Adjust your diet: Reduce meat consumption, especially beef, which has a high carbon footprint. Choose local and seasonal produce.
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle: Minimize waste, avoid single-use plastics, and properly recycle materials according to local guidelines.
  • Make your home efficient: Improve insulation, use energy-efficient appliances, and switch to LED lighting.

The Ripple Effect of Individual Actions

While climate change is a global problem, individual actions collectively create significant impact. When you reduce your carbon footprint, you:

Influence Others

Your sustainable choices can inspire friends, family, and colleagues to adopt greener habits, creating a multiplier effect in your community.

Drive Market Change

Consumer demand for eco-friendly products encourages businesses to develop and offer more sustainable alternatives.

Support Policy Changes

Individuals who practice sustainability are more likely to support environmental policies and vote for leaders who prioritize climate action.
